China

China's education system is renowned for its rigorous academic standards and deep-rooted cultural traditions. China has the largest education system in the world. In June 2024, there were 13.53 million students taking the National Higher Education Entrance Examination - Gao Kao in China.

The Chinese educational structure provides for six years of primary school, three years each of lower secondary school and upper secondary school, and four years in the standard university curriculum. All urban schools are financed by the state, while rural schools depend more heavily on their own financial resources.

On average, primary school pupils spend about seven to eight hours at school whilst a secondary school student spends about twelve to fourteen hours at school if including lunchtime and evening classes. Due to the fierce competitiveness to get into good universities, the pressure to do well for Gao Kao is intense.

 

  • Morning Assembly: Many schools in China start the day with a morning assembly, where students participate in exercises and listen to announcements. This practice helps instill discipline and a sense of community.

  • Large Class Sizes: Classrooms in China can be quite large, with some accommodating up to 50 or more students. Despite this, teachers are highly respected and play a significant role in students' lives.

  • Eye Exercises: Chinese schools have a unique tradition of daily eye exercises to help reduce eye strain and promote eye health. These exercises involve a series of massages and movements around the eyes.
