Ethiopia

Formal education consists of in total 12 grades. Primary school education consists of two cycles: grades 1 to 4 and 5 to 8. Secondary schools also have two cycles: grades 9 to 10 and 11 to 12. Primary schools have over 90% of 7-year-olds enrolled although only about half complete both cycles. This situation varies from one region to the other, being lower in agro-pastoral locations (such as Somali and Afar regions) and the growing regions such as Gambela and Benshangul Gumuz.

Despite significant progress in expanding access to education, Ethiopia faces challenges such as inadequate infrastructure, limited resources, and disparities in educational quality, particularly in rural areas. The government continues to implement initiatives and policies to address these issues and improve educational outcomes for all students.

 

  • Two Educational Systems: Ethiopia has both a traditional religious education system and a modern education system.

  • Long Walks to School: In rural areas, children often have to walk several miles to get to school.

  • Low Literacy Rates: Unfortunately, literacy rates in Ethiopia are quite low, with only around half of the male population being literate and even lower rates for females.

  • High Dropout Rates: Less than half of the students are still enrolled by grade 5, as many children work to help support their families.
